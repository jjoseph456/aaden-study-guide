# 📚 ELA: Synonyms, Antonyms & Context Clues

## Part A: Synonyms & Antonyms

**Question 1 (Synonyms):** Read this sentence: "The sailor was cautious as the storm approached St. Thomas." Which word means the same as *cautious*?

- A) Brave
- B) Careful
- C) Terrified

**Question 2 (Antonyms):** What is the exact opposite of the word *permanent*?

- A) Lasting
- B) Temporary
- C) Broken

---

## Part B: Context Clues

**Question 3:** "The structural damage to the old dock was immense, requiring months of intense labor to repair." Based on the sentence, *immense* means:

- A) Tiny
- B) Standard
- C) Massive

**Question 4 (Spelling):** Choose the sentence with the correct spelling:

- A) Their going to the beach after school.
- B) There going to the beach after school.
- C) They're going to the beach after school.

---

## 🔑 Answer Key

| # | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | B) Careful | Cautious means taking extra care to avoid danger or mistakes |
| 2 | B) Temporary | Permanent means lasting forever; temporary means lasting for only a limited time |
| 3 | C) Massive | The clue is "months of intense labor," which indicates the damage was incredibly large |
| 4 | C) They're | "They're" is the contraction for "they are." "There" specifies a place, and "their" shows ownership |
