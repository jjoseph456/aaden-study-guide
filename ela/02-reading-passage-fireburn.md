# 📖 ELA: Reading Passage & Hidden Meanings

## USVI History Focus: "The Fireburn of Frederiksted"

> **Strategy:** Act like a detective. Look for what the author is *implying* (hinting at) without saying it directly.

---

### The Passage

In October 1878, thirty years after slavery was abolished in the Danish West Indies, the air in St. Croix grew thick with a different kind of heat. Freedom had arrived on paper, but the laborers on the sugar plantations still found themselves locked in invisible chains—bound by laws that forced them into low-paying, multi-year contracts with no right to leave their estates.

Then, a woman named Mary Thomas stepped into the light. Known to history as "Queen Mary," she did not carry official power or military weapons. Instead, she carried a torch and a fierce determination. Alongside other labor leaders, she marched. When the sugar cane fields burned that night, the smoke that filled the Caribbean sky wasn't just a sign of destruction; it was a loud, unavoidable message written in ash that the old ways could no longer stand.

---

### The Quiz

**Question 1 (Vocabulary in Context):** The author writes that the laborers were "locked in invisible chains." What does this phrase really mean?

- A) The laborers were wearing magic, see-through handcuffs.
- B) Even though slavery was over, unfair laws still kept the workers trapped and powerless.
- C) The plantation owners forgot to unlock the gates to the fields.

**Question 2 (Synonyms):** In the passage, the word *abolished* means:

- A) Expanded or made larger.
- B) Forgotten or misplaced.
- C) Ended or officially done away with.

**Question 3 (Inference/Hidden Meaning):** The author notes that the smoke was "a loud, unavoidable message written in ash." What is the hidden meaning?

- A) The workers were trying to use smoke signals to talk to other islands.
- B) The fire was a powerful, physical protest that forced the government to notice their suffering.
- C) The fire accidentally got out of hand because it was a windy night.

---

## 🔑 Answer Key

| # | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | B | The "chains" are a *metaphor* for unfair economic rules that restricted physical and financial freedom |
| 2 | C | To abolish a law, system, or practice means to formally end it |
| 3 | B | The smoke and ash represent the workers making their hidden, systemic struggles visible to the entire island |
