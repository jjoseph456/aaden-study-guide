# 📖 ELA Lesson: Vocabulary Power — Synonyms, Antonyms & Word Relationships

## Why This Matters

In 7th grade, teachers expect you to:
- Know multiple words for the same idea (synonyms)
- Understand opposites instantly (antonyms)
- Figure out unknown words from context (no dictionary needed!)

This makes your writing stronger AND helps on standardized tests.

---

## Synonyms (Words That Mean the Same)

| Common Word | Stronger Synonyms (use these in essays!) |
|-------------|------------------------------------------|
| Big | Enormous, massive, immense, vast |
| Small | Tiny, miniature, minuscule, compact |
| Happy | Elated, joyful, thrilled, ecstatic |
| Sad | Devastated, heartbroken, gloomy, somber |
| Scared | Terrified, frightened, anxious, petrified |
| Said | Stated, declared, whispered, shouted, exclaimed |
| Good | Excellent, outstanding, superb, exceptional |
| Bad | Terrible, awful, dreadful, horrendous |
| Important | Critical, crucial, essential, vital |
| Hard | Challenging, demanding, rigorous, strenuous |

### Pro Tip: Shades of Meaning
Not all synonyms are equal. They have different intensities:

```
Happy → Pleased → Glad → Excited → Thrilled → ECSTATIC
(mild) ————————————————————————————————————→ (extreme)
```

## Antonyms (Exact Opposites)

| Word | Antonym |
|------|---------|
| Permanent | Temporary |
| Ancient | Modern |
| Abundant | Scarce |
| Cautious | Reckless |
| Expand | Contract |
| Generous | Selfish |
| Transparent | Opaque |
| Artificial | Natural |
| Voluntary | Mandatory |
| Triumph | Defeat |

### Prefix Antonyms (Add a prefix to flip the meaning):
- **un-**: happy → unhappy, known → unknown
- **dis-**: agree → disagree, honest → dishonest
- **im-/in-**: possible → impossible, visible → invisible
- **ir-**: responsible → irresponsible, regular → irregular
- **mis-**: understand → misunderstand, lead → mislead

## Word Roots (Unlock Hundreds of Words)

If you know the ROOT, you can guess words you've never seen before:

| Root | Meaning | Examples |
|------|---------|----------|
| aud | hear | audience, auditorium, audio |
| bio | life | biology, biography, biome |
| geo | earth | geography, geology, geometry |
| graph/scrib | write | autograph, describe, scripture |
| port | carry | transport, portable, export |
| rupt | break | erupt, interrupt, corrupt |
| spec/spect | look/see | inspect, spectacle, perspective |
| terra | land/earth | terrain, territory, terrace |
| aqua | water | aquarium, aquatic, aqueduct |
| therm | heat | thermometer, thermal, thermos |

### Example: You see the word "SUBTERRANEAN" for the first time.
- **sub** = under
- **terra** = earth/land
- **-an** = relating to
- **Guess:** Something underground! ✅ (It means underground)

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Replace the Weak Word
Rewrite each sentence using a stronger synonym for the underlined word:

1. The hurricane was really **big**.
2. She was **happy** when she won the science fair.
3. The test was very **hard**.
4. "I don't want to go," he **said** quietly.
5. The sunset over Magens Bay was **good**.

### Exercise 2: Find the Antonym
What is the opposite of:

1. Temporary
2. Expand
3. Generous
4. Ancient
5. Victory

### Exercise 3: Use Roots to Guess
Based on what you know about word roots, what do these words probably mean?

1. Aquatic (aqua = water)
2. Rupture (rupt = break)
3. Spectator (spec = look)
4. Geothermal (geo = earth, therm = heat)
5. Biography (bio = life, graph = write)

---

## ✅ Answers

### Exercise 1 (sample answers):
1. The hurricane was **enormous/devastating**.
2. She was **thrilled/ecstatic** when she won.
3. The test was incredibly **challenging/rigorous**.
4. "I don't want to go," he **whispered/muttered**.
5. The sunset was **breathtaking/spectacular**.

### Exercise 2:
1. Permanent
2. Contract (shrink)
3. Selfish
4. Modern
5. Defeat

### Exercise 3:
1. Aquatic = related to water (living in or near water)
2. Rupture = a break or burst
3. Spectator = someone who watches/looks
4. Geothermal = heat from the earth
5. Biography = a written account of someone's life
