📖 Synonyms, Antonyms & Context

1. "The sailor was cautious as the storm approached St. Thomas." Which word means the same as cautious?

✅ Cautious means taking extra care to avoid danger or mistakes.

2. What is the exact opposite of the word permanent?

✅ Permanent means lasting forever; temporary means lasting for only a limited time.

3. "The structural damage to the old dock was immense, requiring months of intense labor to repair." Immense means:

✅ The clue is "months of intense labor," which indicates the damage was incredibly large.

4. Choose the sentence with the correct spelling:

✅ "They're" is short for "they are." "There" is a place. "Their" shows ownership.

📖 The Fireburn of Frederiksted

Read carefully, then answer the questions below.

In October 1878, thirty years after slavery was abolished in the Danish West Indies, the air in St. Croix grew thick with a different kind of heat. Freedom had arrived on paper, but the laborers on the sugar plantations still found themselves locked in invisible chains—bound by laws that forced them into low-paying, multi-year contracts with no right to leave their estates.

Then, a woman named Mary Thomas stepped into the light. Known to history as "Queen Mary," she did not carry official power or military weapons. Instead, she carried a torch and a fierce determination. Alongside other labor leaders, she marched. When the sugar cane fields burned that night, the smoke that filled the Caribbean sky wasn't just a sign of destruction; it was a loud, unavoidable message written in ash that the old ways could no longer stand.

1. The author writes the laborers were "locked in invisible chains." What does this phrase really mean?

✅ The "chains" are a metaphor for unfair economic rules that restricted freedom.

2. In the passage, the word abolished means:

✅ To abolish something means to formally and completely end it.

3. The smoke was "a loud, unavoidable message written in ash." What is the hidden meaning?

✅ The ash and smoke represent making invisible problems visible to the entire island.

🧮 Math Diagnostic Quiz

1. (6th Grade) What is 3/4 ÷ 1/2?

✅ Keep, Change, Flip: 3/4 × 2/1 = 6/4 = 3/2

2. (6th Grade) Solve 1.25 × 4

✅ 125 × 4 = 500, then place the decimal: 5.00 = 5

3. (6th Grade) A recipe uses 2 cups of sugar for 5 cups of flour. What is the ratio of sugar to flour?

✅ Ratios compare quantities in the order they are asked: sugar TO flour = 2:5

4. (6th Grade) Simplify: 3(x + 4)

✅ Distributive property: multiply 3 by both x AND 4.

5. (7th Grade) Solve: -3 + (-5)

✅ Adding a negative = moving further left on the number line: -3 + (-5) = -8

6. (7th Grade) If 2x + 5 = 13, what is x?

✅ Subtract 5 from both sides: 2x = 8. Divide by 2: x = 4.

7. (7th Grade) If 3 notebooks cost $6, how much do 7 notebooks cost?

✅ Unit rate: $6 ÷ 3 = $2 each. Then $2 × 7 = $14.

8. (7th Grade) A ferry travels 15 miles in 30 minutes. What is its speed per hour?

✅ Double the 30-minute distance: 15 × 2 = 30 miles per hour.

🔬 Science Diagnostic Quiz

1. (6th Grade) What organelle is known as the "powerhouse" of the cell?

✅ The mitochondria generates the cell's chemical energy (ATP).

2. (6th Grade) What is the primary source of energy for most ecosystems on Earth?

✅ The Sun provides radiation that plants use in photosynthesis, starting the food chain.

3. (6th Grade) Name the three primary layers of the Earth:

✅ The three structural layers are the crust (outer), mantle (middle), and core (inner).

4. (6th Grade) Water turning from a liquid into a gas is called:

✅ Evaporation is the phase change from liquid to gas.

5. (7th Grade) What is the difference between an atom and a molecule?

✅ Atoms are individual elements. When atoms bond together, they form molecules.

6. (7th Grade) What happens at a convergent plate boundary?

✅ Convergent = plates colliding, pushing land up into mountains or causing quakes.

7. (7th Grade) What happens to water molecules when liquid water boils into steam?

✅ Heat energy makes molecules move faster, breaking bonds and spreading apart.

8. (7th Grade) Plants use sunlight, water, and CO₂ to make food. This process is called:

✅ Photo (light) + synthesis (making) = making food from light.
