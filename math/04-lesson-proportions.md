# 🧮 Math Lesson: Proportions & Unit Rates

## What Is a Proportion?

A proportion says that **two ratios are equal**:

```
2/3 = 4/6    ← These are proportional (same relationship)
```

Think of it as scaling a recipe up or down — the relationship stays the same.

---

## Cross-Multiplication (The Shortcut)

To solve a proportion, **cross-multiply**:

```
2/3 = x/12

2 × 12 = 3 × x
24 = 3x
x = 8
```

**Visual:**
```
  2     x
 --- = ---
  3    12

  2 × 12 = 3 × x  (cross multiply: make an X)
```

---

## Unit Rates (The "Per One" Number)

A **unit rate** tells you the amount per ONE unit. It makes comparing things easy.

### How to Find a Unit Rate:
Divide to get "per 1":

- 6 mangos for $12 → $12 ÷ 6 = **$2 per mango**
- 150 miles in 3 hours → 150 ÷ 3 = **50 mph**
- 24 questions in 40 minutes → 24 ÷ 40 = **0.6 questions per minute**

---

## Real-Life Examples (Caribbean Edition 🌊)

### Example 1: Better Deal?
- Store A: 5 coconuts for $8
- Store B: 3 coconuts for $5

Find unit rates:
- Store A: $8 ÷ 5 = **$1.60 per coconut**
- Store B: $5 ÷ 3 = **$1.67 per coconut**

**Store A is the better deal!**

### Example 2: Scale Drawing
A map of St. Thomas uses the scale: 1 inch = 4 miles.

If Charlotte Amalie to Red Hook measures 3.5 inches on the map, what's the real distance?

```
1/4 = 3.5/x
1 × x = 4 × 3.5
x = 14 miles
```

### Example 3: Cooking
A recipe for 4 servings of fish tacos uses 3 cups of flour. You need to make 10 servings. How much flour?

```
4/3 = 10/x
4x = 30
x = 7.5 cups of flour
```

## Proportional vs. Non-Proportional

**Proportional:** The ratio stays the same AND passes through (0, 0).
- 2 hours = $20, 4 hours = $40, 6 hours = $60 ✅ (always $10/hr)

**Non-Proportional:** Has a starting fee or the rate changes.
- Taxi: $5 base + $2/mile ❌ (that $5 base breaks it)

## Practice Problems

1. Solve: 3/5 = x/20
2. Solve: 4/x = 8/14
3. A car drives 180 miles on 6 gallons of gas. What's the unit rate (miles per gallon)?
4. Which is faster: 200 meters in 25 seconds, or 150 meters in 20 seconds?
5. A recipe uses 2 cups of rice for 6 people. How much rice for 15 people?
6. On a map, 2 cm = 5 km. If two towns are 8 cm apart on the map, how far apart are they really?
7. You earn $45 for 5 hours of work. How much for 8 hours?
