# 🔬 Science: Diagnostic Quiz

## Part A: 6th-Grade Mastery Review

**Question 1 (Cell Biology):** What organelle is known as the "powerhouse" of the cell?

**Question 2 (Ecosystems):** What is the primary source of energy for most ecosystems on Earth?

**Question 3 (Earth Science):** Name the three primary layers of the Earth.

**Question 4 (Water Cycle):** Water turning from a liquid into a gas and rising into the atmosphere is called what?

---

## Part B: 7th-Grade Sneak Peek

**Question 5 (Chemistry):** What is the difference between an atom and a molecule?

**Question 6 (Plate Tectonics):** What geological event typically happens at a convergent plate boundary?

**Question 7 (Physical Science):** What happens to water molecules when liquid water boils into steam?

**Question 8 (Plant Biology):** Plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to make food. What is this chemical process called?

---

## 🔑 Answer Key & Explanations

| # | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Mitochondria | The organelle responsible for generating cellular chemical energy |
| 2 | The Sun | Provides the radiation necessary to kickstart photosynthesis in primary producers |
| 3 | Crust, mantle, and core | The three primary structural layers of Earth |
| 4 | Evaporation | The phase transition from liquid water to gaseous vapor |
| 5 | An atom is a single building block; a molecule is two or more atoms bonded together | Atoms combine to form molecules |
| 6 | Mountains form, or earthquakes and volcanic activity occur | Caused by tectonic plates colliding |
| 7 | They speed up and spread further apart | Thermal energy overcomes molecular bonds |
| 8 | Photosynthesis | The chemical synthesis of organic compounds using light energy |
