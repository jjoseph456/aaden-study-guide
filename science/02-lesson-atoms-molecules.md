# 🔬 Science Lesson: Atoms, Molecules & Chemical Reactions

## The Building Blocks of Everything

Everything around you — the ocean, your phone, the air you breathe — is made of **atoms**.

---

## What Is an Atom?

An atom is the **smallest unit of an element** that still has the properties of that element.

### Parts of an Atom:

```
        ☁️ Electron Cloud (negative -)
       /
  ⚛️  ●  Nucleus (center)
       \    ├── Protons (positive +)
        \   └── Neutrons (neutral, no charge)
```

| Part | Charge | Location |
|------|--------|----------|
| Proton | Positive (+) | Inside the nucleus |
| Neutron | Neutral (0) | Inside the nucleus |
| Electron | Negative (−) | Orbiting outside the nucleus |

**Key fact:** The number of **protons** determines what element it is.
- 1 proton = Hydrogen
- 6 protons = Carbon
- 8 protons = Oxygen

---

## What Is a Molecule?

A **molecule** is two or more atoms bonded (stuck) together.

### Examples:
- **H₂O** (Water) = 2 Hydrogen atoms + 1 Oxygen atom
- **O₂** (Oxygen gas) = 2 Oxygen atoms bonded together
- **CO₂** (Carbon dioxide) = 1 Carbon + 2 Oxygen atoms
- **NaCl** (Table salt) = 1 Sodium + 1 Chlorine

> The little number after each letter tells you HOW MANY of that atom are in the molecule.

---

## Elements vs. Compounds

| Term | Definition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Element | Made of only ONE type of atom | Gold (Au), Oxygen (O₂) |
| Compound | Two or more DIFFERENT elements bonded together | Water (H₂O), Salt (NaCl) |
| Mixture | Things mixed together but NOT bonded | Saltwater, trail mix |

---

## Chemical Reactions: When Atoms Rearrange

A **chemical reaction** happens when atoms break apart and reconnect in a new way to form different substances.

### Signs a Chemical Reaction Happened:
1. 🎨 Color change
2. 💨 Gas produced (bubbles)
3. 🌡️ Temperature change (gets hot or cold)
4. 💡 Light produced
5. �ite New substance formed (can't easily undo it)

### Examples You See Every Day:
- **Rusting:** Iron + Oxygen → Iron oxide (rust)
- **Cooking an egg:** Heat changes the protein permanently
- **Burning wood:** Wood + Oxygen → Ash + CO₂ + Heat
- **Vinegar + Baking soda:** Bubbles! (CO₂ gas is produced)

### What Does NOT Count:
- Melting ice (still H₂O, just changed form)
- Dissolving sugar in water (sugar is still sugar)
- Cutting paper (same substance, just smaller)

These are **physical changes** — the substance stays the same.

## The Periodic Table (Your Cheat Sheet)

The periodic table organizes ALL known elements by their number of protons.

Key groups to know:
- **Metals** (left side): Shiny, conduct electricity, malleable
- **Nonmetals** (right side): Dull, poor conductors, brittle
- **Noble Gases** (far right column): Don't react with anything (they're "full")

---

## Real-Life Connections (Island Life 🏝️)

1. **Salt in ocean water:** NaCl dissolved in H₂O (a mixture — you can separate them by evaporating the water)
2. **Rust on boats:** Iron + oxygen + salt water = faster rusting (chemical reaction)
3. **Breathing:** You inhale O₂, your cells use it, you exhale CO₂ (chemical reaction in your body)
4. **Sunscreen:** Chemicals in sunscreen absorb UV radiation before it damages your skin cells

## Practice Questions

1. How many atoms are in one molecule of H₂O?
2. Is dissolving salt in water a chemical or physical change? Why?
3. What are the three parts of an atom?
4. A nail left outside turns orange-brown. Is this a chemical or physical change? What's your evidence?
5. What's the difference between an element and a compound?
6. Carbon dioxide is CO₂. How many carbon atoms and how many oxygen atoms are in one molecule?
7. You mix vinegar and baking soda and see bubbles. Is this chemical or physical? How do you know?

---

## ✅ Answers

| # | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 1 | 3 atoms total (2 hydrogen + 1 oxygen) |
| 2 | Physical change — salt is still salt, water is still water. You can get the salt back by evaporating |
| 3 | Protons (positive), Neutrons (neutral), Electrons (negative) |
| 4 | Chemical change — new substance (rust/iron oxide) formed, color changed, can't reverse it easily |
| 5 | An element is one type of atom only. A compound is two or more different elements bonded together |
| 6 | 1 carbon atom and 2 oxygen atoms |
| 7 | Chemical change — gas (CO₂ bubbles) is produced, which is a new substance |
