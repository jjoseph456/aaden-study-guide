# 🔬 Science Lesson: Plate Tectonics & Earth's Structure

## Earth's Layers (From Outside In)

Imagine cutting the Earth in half like an avocado:

```
🌍 Cross-Section:

  ┌─────────────────────────┐
  │  CRUST (thin outer shell)│  ← 5-70 km thick (where we live!)
  │  ┌───────────────────┐  │
  │  │  MANTLE (hot rock) │  │  ← 2,900 km thick (slowly flows)
  │  │  ┌─────────────┐  │  │
  │  │  │ OUTER CORE  │  │  │  ← Liquid iron/nickel
  │  │  │  ┌───────┐  │  │  │
  │  │  │  │ INNER │  │  │  │  ← Solid iron (incredibly hot!)
  │  │  │  │ CORE  │  │  │  │
  │  │  │  └───────┘  │  │  │
  │  │  └─────────────┘  │  │
  │  └───────────────────┘  │
  └─────────────────────────┘
```

| Layer | State | Temperature | Fun Fact |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Crust | Solid | Cool (surface) | Thinner under oceans, thicker under mountains |
| Mantle | Semi-solid (flows slowly) | 1000-3700°C | Makes up 84% of Earth's volume |
| Outer Core | Liquid | 4000-5000°C | Creates Earth's magnetic field |
| Inner Core | Solid | 5000-6000°C | As hot as the Sun's surface! |

## Tectonic Plates: Earth's Moving Puzzle

The crust is broken into huge pieces called **tectonic plates** that float on the mantle like crackers floating on thick soup.

There are about **15 major plates**. They move 1-10 cm per year (about as fast as your fingernails grow).

**Why do they move?** Hot mantle rock rises, cools, and sinks in circular patterns called **convection currents** — like a lava lamp.

---

## Three Types of Plate Boundaries

### 1. Convergent (Plates Crash Together) 💥
- Plates push INTO each other
- **Result:** Mountains, volcanoes, earthquakes, ocean trenches
- **Example:** The Himalayas (India crashed into Asia)
- **Caribbean connection:** The Caribbean plate pushing against the North American plate causes earthquakes in the Virgin Islands

### 2. Divergent (Plates Pull Apart) ↔️
- Plates move AWAY from each other
- **Result:** New crust forms, mid-ocean ridges, rift valleys
- **Example:** Mid-Atlantic Ridge (the Atlantic Ocean is getting wider!)
- **Caribbean connection:** The Mid-Atlantic Ridge runs through the Atlantic near the islands

### 3. Transform (Plates Slide Past Each Other) ↕️
- Plates slide SIDEWAYS alongside each other
- **Result:** Earthquakes (no volcanoes or mountains)
- **Example:** San Andreas Fault in California

---

## Evidence That Plates Move

How do scientists KNOW the continents moved?

1. **Puzzle Fit:** South America and Africa look like puzzle pieces that fit together
2. **Matching Fossils:** Same ancient animal fossils found on continents now separated by oceans
3. **Matching Rocks:** Same rock types and mountain chains line up across oceans
4. **Seafloor Spreading:** New crust is constantly being made at mid-ocean ridges
5. **GPS Measurements:** We can actually measure plates moving today!

## The Rock Cycle

Rocks constantly change form over millions of years:

```
    IGNEOUS (cooled magma/lava)
       ↗️          ↘️
  Melting        Weathering & Erosion
     ↑                ↓
 METAMORPHIC ←── SEDIMENTARY
  (heat & pressure   (layers compressed)
   underground)
```

| Rock Type | How It Forms | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Igneous | Melted rock (magma/lava) cools and hardens | Granite, obsidian, basalt |
| Sedimentary | Layers of sand/mud/shells pile up and compress over time | Sandstone, limestone |
| Metamorphic | Existing rock is changed by extreme heat and pressure | Marble (was limestone), slate (was shale) |

---

## Caribbean Connection 🌴

- The **Virgin Islands** sit on the Caribbean Plate
- **Earthquakes** happen because the Caribbean plate grinds against the North American plate (convergent boundary)
- The **volcanic islands** (like Montserrat, St. Kitts) formed from subduction — one plate diving under another, melting, and erupting as volcanoes
- St. Thomas is made of **igneous and metamorphic rock** from ancient volcanic activity

## ✅ Answers
